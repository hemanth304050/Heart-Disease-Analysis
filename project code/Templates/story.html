Chapter 2 · Key Findings
Age Is a Critical Risk Factor

Patients aged 50–60 show the highest incidence of heart disease. Risk compounds with age, particularly for males, who represent the majority of positive cases.

Cholesterol & Blood Pressure

Patients with cholesterol above 240 mg/dl had a significantly higher probability of heart disease. Resting blood pressure above 130 mmHg further elevates risk.

Chest Pain Type Matters

Asymptomatic chest pain (type 0) is paradoxically the highest-risk category, suggesting patients may be unaware of underlying cardiac stress.

Max Heart Rate as Protective Factor

A higher maximum heart rate achieved during exercise correlates with lower disease risk, indicating better cardiovascular fitness.

Chapter 3 · Machine Learning Insights

We trained 4 machine learning models — Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and SVM — to predict heart disease from the 13 clinical features.

The Gradient Boosting and Random Forest models consistently achieved the highest accuracy and AUC scores, demonstrating that ensemble methods capture complex nonlinear relationships in cardiac data effectively.

The most predictive features identified were: thalach (max heart rate), ca (vessel count), oldpeak (ST depression), and chest pain type.

Chapter 4 · Recommendations
  • Screen high-risk groups (age 50+, male, asymptomatic) proactively.
  • Monitor cholesterol — target below 200 mg/dl through diet and medication.
  • Manage blood pressure — keep resting BP below 120 mmHg.
  • Encourage exercise — sustained aerobic activity improves heart rate capacity.
  • Deploy the ML predictor in outpatient clinics for early alert systems.
